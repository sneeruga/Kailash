My Journey to Mount Kailash

Mount Kailash is a massive black rock which is located in the Himalayas. At 22,000 feet, Mount Kailash is not only a mountain, but also a very sacred place for people of all religions including Hinduism, Jainism, and Buddhism. Hindus believe that the holy mountain is the abode of Lord Shiva, the God of destruction. You might be thinking, "Destruction? There's a God for that?" Yes, indeed there is. Destruction is essential because it opens up the opportunity for new creation. For a Hindu, the strenuous journey to Mount Kailash for darshan is done to wash away all sins and be released from delusion and ignorance.

Walking around the mountain known as, parikrama, is no easy task. Lower levels of oxygen and altitude sickness kick in while you tread around the mountain.

Manasarovar is a lake which symbolizes purity. according to Hindu religion, the lake was created by Lord Bramha, the God of creation.
